Smart Home & Automation

Smart home, but private and reliable.

Most smart home systems require cloud services and break when the internet goes down. We set up local-first automation that works offline, respects your privacy, and gives you full control.

What platforms we use (Home Assistant and more)

We prefer Home Assistant for smart home automation because it runs entirely on your network. Everything happens locally—no cloud required. That means your automations work even when the internet is down, and your data stays in your home.

Home Assistant integrates with hundreds of devices and platforms, from Zigbee and Z-Wave to Wi-Fi devices, smart locks, cameras, and more.

Examples: lighting, locks, presence, notifications

  • Lighting scenes: "Movie mode" dims all lights, "Good morning" gradually brightens bedroom lights.
  • Locks and garage: Automatically lock doors when you leave, open garage when you arrive.
  • Presence automations: Turn on lights when you arrive home, adjust thermostat based on who's home.
  • Notifications: Get alerts when cameras detect motion, when doors open, or when the washer finishes.

Privacy and local-first approach

Most smart home platforms require cloud services. With Home Assistant, your automations run locally, your data stays in your home, and you can still access everything remotely via VPN if you want. It's the best of both worlds: convenience without sacrificing privacy or reliability.

Good fit if you:

  • Want to automate lights, locks, climate, and more.
  • Prefer local control over cloud-dependent systems.
  • Have lots of smart devices that need coordination.
  • Want automations that work even when internet is down.
