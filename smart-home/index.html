Smart Home & Automation

Smart home, but private and reliable.

Most smart home systems require cloud services and break when the internet goes down. We set up local-first automation that works offline, respects your privacy, and gives you full control.

What platforms/tools we use (Home Assistant and more)

We prefer Home Assistant for smart home automation because it runs entirely on your network. Everything happens locally—no cloud required. That means your automations work even when the internet is down, and your data stays in your home.

Home Assistant integrates with hundreds of devices and platforms, from Zigbee and Z-Wave to Wi-Fi devices, smart locks, cameras, and more. It's powerful enough for complex automations but can be kept simple if you just want the basics.

We'll also work with other platforms if you prefer—Hubitat, OpenHAB, or even simpler setups using just HomeKit or Google Home. The key is choosing the right tool for your needs and comfort level.

Examples: lighting scenes, locks/garage, presence automations, notifications

Here are some common automations we set up:

  • Lighting scenes: "Movie mode" dims all lights, "Good morning" gradually brightens bedroom lights, "Away" turns everything off.
  • Locks and garage: Automatically lock doors when you leave, open garage when you arrive, send notifications if doors are left unlocked.
  • Presence automations: Turn on lights when you arrive home, adjust thermostat based on who's home, pause music when you leave.
  • Notifications: Get alerts when cameras detect motion, when doors open, when the washer finishes, or any other event you care about.
  • Climate control: Adjust temperature based on time of day, occupancy, or weather conditions.

The best automations are the ones you set and forget. We'll design automations that make your home feel smarter without requiring constant tweaking.

Integration with the network and NAS

Smart home devices work best when they're part of a well-designed network. We'll put IoT devices on a separate VLAN so they can't access your main network, configure proper firewall rules, and ensure your network can handle the traffic without slowing down.

If you have a NAS, we can integrate it with your smart home setup. Store camera footage on the NAS, use the NAS for Home Assistant backups, or even run Home Assistant on the NAS if it's powerful enough.

Everything works together: your network provides the foundation, your NAS stores data, and your smart home automations make it all feel seamless.

Privacy and local-first approach

Most smart home platforms require cloud services. That means your data goes to someone else's servers, and your automations break when the internet goes down. We prefer local-first setups where everything runs on your network.

With Home Assistant and similar platforms, your automations run locally, your data stays in your home, and you can still access everything remotely via VPN if you want. It's the best of both worlds: convenience without sacrificing privacy or reliability.

  • Prefer local control over cloud-dependent systems.
  • Have lots of smart devices that need coordination.
  • Want automations that work even when the internet is down.
